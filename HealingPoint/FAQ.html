FAQ

What is acupucture?

Acupuncture is the procedure of inserting and manipulating needles in specific areas of the body based on Traditional Oriental Medicine Meridian Theory. Acupuncture treats illness simply by restoring balance withing the body and promoting its natural healing abilities.

What are meridians?

Meridians can be likened to a road map connecting various areas within the body. Acupuncture meridians have shown to overlap with conventional medicine's neurological system, blood vessels, lymphatic system, and muscular fascia.

Does acupuncture hurt?

Needles used in acupuncture are more than 10x smaller than hypodermic needles used in western medicine. Acupuncture needles are approximately the width of a human hair! They are therefore far less painful. In fact, most patients report feeling relaxed during and after an acupuncture treatment.

How does Chinese Herbal Medicine work?

Chinese Herbal Medicine is comprised of over 365 herbs. Each individual herb is understood by its nature, taste, temperature, and the meridians it enters. Herbs are the precursors for pharmaceutical drugs but offer less side effects since they are kept in their whole form. Chinese herbs consist of the leaf, stem, flower, or root of a plant as well as some minerals and animal products. A formula is a combination of herbs tailored for each individual based on their Oriental Medicine diagnosis. Each ingredient is purposefully used to create a balanced interaction within a given formula. Herbal therapy can be used alone or in conjunction with acupuncture treatments.
